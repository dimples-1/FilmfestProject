The Storytellers Behind the Screen

Film Festival 2025 proudly showcases a talented roster of filmmakers who bring bold visions, cultural voices, and compelling narratives to life. From emerging creators to seasoned directors, these individuals redefine what it means to tell a story through film.

Featured Filmmakers

Ariana Cruz

Film: “Echoes of Silence”
Genre: Drama / Psychological
Country: Philippines

Bio:
Ariana Cruz is a Manila-based independent filmmaker whose work focuses on emotional realism and inner conflict. Her latest film, Echoes of Silence, explores the story of a young girl navigating trauma and healing in the shadow of family expectations. Ariana graduated from the University of the Philippines with a degree in Film and has previously worked in documentary production.

“Film is my way of showing the quiet battles people fight every day. This story is deeply personal—and I hope audiences find a piece of themselves in it.”

Julian Reyes

Film: “The 5th Stop”
Genre: Mystery / Short Film
Country: Philippines

Bio:
Julian Reyes is a 22-year-old student filmmaker from Cebu who blends surrealism and everyday stories into thought-provoking narratives. His short film, The 5th Stop, follows a night-shift jeepney driver who begins to see ghostly figures among his passengers—raising questions about regret, grief, and fate. This is Julian's second short film to be featured in a national festival.

“I wanted to use the ordinary—like a jeepney ride—and turn it into something eerie but meaningful. Sometimes horror is just a memory refusing to be forgotten.”

Mika Tanaka

Film: “Letters from Tomorrow”
Genre: Sci-Fi / Romance
Country: Japan

Bio:
Mika Tanaka is a Tokyo-based filmmaker known for her poetic approach to sci-fi. In Letters from Tomorrow, she tells the story of two people in different timelines who communicate through mysterious letters that appear in a shared mailbox. The film blends nostalgia with futuristic themes, questioning the boundaries of time and love.

“Technology and emotion are often seen as opposites, but I love mixing them in stories. This film is a love letter—literally and figuratively.”
